Intellectual Development

Language

  • Verbally communicating and listening to others
  • Dramatic play
  • Listening to and telling stories
  • Beginning to identify meaningful words – own name, friends’ names, calendar words, job chart, etc.
  • Dictating, drawing, and writing stories, and creating books of the child’s own making

Math

  • Identifying shapes, sizes, patterns, and sequences
  • Sorting and categorizing into sets
  • Counting and comparison, one-to-one correspondence
  • Measuring length and weight
  • Using numbers (as symbols and to represent quantity)
  • Graphing

Science

  • Observing the weather, plant life, and people
  • Observing and/or collecting plants, animals, and insects in their environment
  • Learning basic nature conservation as well as concept and practice of recycling materials and goods
  • Growing plants, observing the stages of growth, and indetifying the parts
  • Exploring animals through hatching chicken eggs, butterflies, tadpoles, and more
  • Learing about how magnets work

Social Sciences

  • Learning differences and simliarities between self and others.
  • Inviting family members to share cultural item, stories; teach words in other languages; or read a book to a the class
  • Exploring the St. Louis community through field trips
  • Learning about different occupations through book and vists from people holding a variety of jobs
  • Doing hands-on activities with cultural materials
  • Learning about people with disablities and they daily lives and learning about how to repspect each person as an individual
  • Role-playing about families, occupations, abilities, or traditions.
